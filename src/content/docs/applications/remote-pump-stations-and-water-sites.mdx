# Remote Pump Stations and Water Sites

These sites reward simple, durable architectures. Power can be limited, access windows are sparse, and reliability matters more than feature density. The correct design often comes from field survivability and serviceability before it comes from radio preference.

## Typical pressures

- Intermittent or constrained power budgets.
- Uneven network coverage or backhaul uncertainty.
- Limited opportunities for on-site troubleshooting.

## Design implications

- Local buffering becomes more important when backhaul is unstable.
- Enclosure, antenna, and power design can matter as much as the gateway itself.
- Hardware categories should be chosen for serviceability, not only capability density.
